## Khan Academy's AI Tutor Khanmigo

Sal Khan demonstrates Khanmigo, Khan Academy's new AI-powered tutor, showing how it integrates into existing Khan Academy content and introduces entirely new learning activities. The tool appears as a small bot icon on exercises, videos, and articles, offering contextual help without simply giving answers away.

## Socratic Tutoring in Action

The demo showcases Khanmigo's Socratic tutoring approach through a math distribution problem. When Sal deliberately makes an error distributing a negative sign, Khanmigo responds with thoughtful feedback: "make sure you distribute the negative 2 to both terms in the parentheses." Rather than solving problems for students, it guides them to discover answers independently.

This same tutoring capability works across all Khan Academy subjects and content types, from exercises to videos to articles. When asked to write a poem about a math video, Khanmigo produces a coherent poem referencing the actual content from that specific video.

Khanmigo also personalizes learning by connecting to student interests. When asked why math matters, it tailors the answer to soccer, explaining how equations apply to player performance analytics, game statistics, and training optimization.

## Safety and Privacy Features

- Every student conversation displays: "this conversation is recorded and viewable by your teacher"
- Parent visibility also available
- Parents and teachers receive notifications if students attempt potentially harmful activities
- Khan Academy explicitly states it does not pass personally identifiable information to the AI
- Does not use student data for training
- Activity guardrails prevent students from using the tool for cheating while still enabling genuine learning

## New Activity Types

The demo highlights multiple entirely new activity categories beyond existing Khan Academy content:

- **Tutor me** — Conversational tutoring on any subject from entropy to history
- **Ignite your curiosity** — Discussion questions on topics like world history, allowing follow-up questions and rabbit holes
- **Explore with a historical figure** — Simulates conversations with figures like George Washington, responding authentically to questions about challenges, doubt, and decision-making
- **Debate AI** — Students can debate AI on topics from elementary questions about zoos to high school debates on student debt cancellation, with Khanmigo presenting counterarguments to whichever side the student takes
- **Literary character chats** — Students interact with figures like Zeus, who maintains character while discussing divine concerns and asking students about their own challenges

## Teacher Tools

For teachers, Khanmigo generates lesson plans including hooks, exit tickets, and complete lesson structures. Sal demonstrates requesting a ninth-grade Civil War lesson, receiving a full draft with a Socratic opening question: "do you think the Civil War was inevitable why or why not." Teachers can specify preferences for project-based, hands-on, or discussion-focused approaches.

A separate teacher refresh feature lets educators quiz themselves on upcoming topics to ensure they have solid subject command before teaching.

## Rollout and Availability

Khanmigo is currently in pilot testing with teachers and students co-designing the experience. Khan Academy is establishing a waiting list for broader access, with prioritization given to donors who fund the research and development, and to schools already part of the Khan Academy District offering.
