# Junior Engineer Advantage

**Summary**: Mihail Eric argues that junior developers have a structural advantage in adopting AI-native skills because they arrive as "sponges" unconstrained by ingrained industry habits, combining "good naivety" with healthy technical confidence.

**Sources**: [[raw/mihail-eric-junior-engineers-crisis.md|Mihail Eric Stanford talk]]

---

## The Paradox

Senior developers often resist AI tools because 20 years of ingrained habits make them "stubborn about alternatives." Junior developers arrive as "sponges" — everything is possible to them, unconstrained by internalized beliefs about how industries work.

Eric calls this **"good naivety"** — perfectly suited for startup founders willing to tackle problems others see as unsolvable.

## Two Key Advantages

### 1. Good Naivety
- Not constrained by "how things are done"
- Open to trying AI-native approaches without resistance
- Unconstrained by internalized industry beliefs

### 2. Healthy "Arrogance"
- Confidence that any problem has a software solution
- Willingness to dig into internals when things break
- Not simply moving away from hard problems

## Why This Matters for AI Adoption

The combination of:
- Nimbleness
- Experimentation
- Stubborn problem-solving

...makes junior developers uniquely suited to adopt AI-native skills fastest, even if employment feels harder to obtain initially.

Harvard Business School professor Rem Coning adds that the future belongs to those who can **allocate intelligence effectively** — embedding AI directly into products to remove humans from the loop entirely.
