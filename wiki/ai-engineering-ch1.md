# AI Engineering Chapter 1 — Introduction to Building AI Applications with Foundation Models

**Summary**: Chip Huyen introduces AI engineering as a discipline built on foundation models, tracing the evolution from language models to LLM to foundation models to AI engineering, and mapping the consumer/enterprise use case landscape.

**Sources**: [[raw/AI Engineer Chapter 1 (Chip Huyen, 2025).md|AI Engineering Chapter 1]]

---

## Key Points

### Evolution of AI
Language Models → LLMs (self-supervision) → Foundation Models (multimodal) → AI Engineering

### Two Goals of the Chapter
1. Explain AI engineering as a discipline, enabled by foundation models
2. Overview of the process for building applications on foundation models

### AI Engineering Is Still Early
Despite many AI applications in production, we're in the early stages with countless more innovations to be built.

### Pre-Build Question
An important yet often overlooked question: **should you build it?** before how to build it.

## The AI Engineering Stack

AI engineering evolved from ML engineering. Many ML engineering principles still apply, but AI engineering brings new challenges and solutions. The stack has changed from traditional ML engineering.

## Overwhelm and Framework

AI is great at aggregating information, but tools can only help to a certain extent. The more overwhelming the space, the more important a framework becomes. This book aims to provide that framework for AI engineering.
