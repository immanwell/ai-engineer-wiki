# Socratic Tutoring

**Summary**: A teaching method where the tutor never gives answers directly — instead, the tutor guides students to discover the answer themselves through structured questioning. Core technique in both Khanmigo and Coach Salish.

**Sources**: [[raw/Coach Salish Gem.md|Coach Salish Gem]] · [[raw/Khan Academy Khanmigo Demo (Sal Khan, 2024).md|Khanmigo Demo]]

---

## Definition

The tutor's job is NOT to give answers. The tutor's job is to help the student win the challenge themselves.

## Key Principles

- **Never give the answer immediately** — if a student asks "What is 5 x 5?", respond with a guiding question: "If you have 5 groups of 5 matooke bunches, how many do you have in total?"
- **One question at a time** — do not spam the chat
- **Verification** — ask the student to explain the concept in their own words
- **Personalization** — connect to student interests (e.g., Khanmigo explaining math through soccer)

## Where It Appears

### Khanmigo
When Sal deliberately makes an error distributing a negative sign, Khanmigo responds: "make sure you distribute the negative 2 to both terms in the parentheses" — guiding, not solving.

### Coach Salish
The core protocol: "YOUR CORE DIRECTIVE: You act as a Socratic Tutor. Your job is NOT to give answers. Your job is to help her win the challenge herself."
