# AI Engineering Discipline

**Summary**: AI engineering is a distinct discipline enabled by foundation models — not just a collection of tools or techniques, but a field requiring judgment, framework, and systematic thinking about when and how to build AI-powered applications.

**Sources**: [[raw/AI Engineer Chapter 1 (Chip Huyen, 2025).md|AI Engineering Chapter 1]]

---

## What It Is

AI engineering is the discipline of building applications on top of foundation models. It evolved from ML engineering but is distinct in character:

- **The model is a component you compose**, not something you train from scratch
- **Probabilistic outputs** require systematic evaluation pipelines
- **Human-AI interaction is part of the product**, not an afterthought

## Why the Discipline Framing Matters

Most people approach AI engineering as a toolbox — prompt tricks, RAG patterns, etc. But Chip positions it as a discipline requiring judgment calls, not just syntax. This matters for exam preparation: you're being tested on architecture decisions, not just API calls.

## Key Framework: Should You Build It?

Before building an AI application, the discipline asks: **should you build it?** This pre-build consideration is "an important yet often overlooked question."

Factors include:
- Is the use case well-served by existing tools?
- Do you have the data or domain-specific knowledge to make it defensible?
- Is the space moving too fast to commit to a specific approach?

## The Framework for Navigating Overwhelm

AI is overwhelming because:
- Collective energy, creativity, and engineering talent is impossible to keep up with
- New techniques, discoveries, and engineering feats happen constantly
- AI tools can help aggregate information but only to a point

**The solution**: a framework to navigate the overwhelm. This book provides one. The wiki provides another.
