# Software Taste

**Summary**: Eric defines "taste" as the drive to go beyond functional correctness into exceptional territory — specifically, what he calls "the last mile" where you build something more complex because you genuinely want to solve a problem, not just get a grade.

**Sources**: [[raw/mihail-eric-junior-engineers-crisis.md|Mihail Eric Stanford talk]]

---

## Definition

Taste separates good software from great software. Eric traces it to what he calls **"the last mile"** — after achieving 100% on an assignment, choosing to build something more complex because you want to solve a problem, not just get a grade.

## How Taste Develops

The students who excelled most in Eric's experience:
- Built startups around their class projects
- Continued long after the course ended
- Saw genuine potential in problems they were solving

## Taste and AI

Eric describes how even teams at Anthropic constantly rewrite Claude using Claude itself — treating their own product as a laboratory for experimentation. This is taste in action: not settling for "good enough" when "exceptional" is possible.

## The Intuition Factor

Eric reinforces that while he can offer guidance, each developer must **"beat their head against the wall" personally** to develop genuine intuition for what works. Taste can't be taught directly — it develops through struggle and deep engagement with hard problems.
